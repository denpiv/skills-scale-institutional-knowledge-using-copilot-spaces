# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers are responsible for designing user experiences, conducting user research, and ensuring product usability. They translate user needs and business goals into intuitive interfaces and flows.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research, interviews, and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers to align design with product goals
- Work with Developers to ensure accurate implementation of designs
- Review and advise on acceptance criteria related to UX and usability
- Maintain and contribute to the design system

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive and accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Ensure design consistency across features and releases

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- UX research summaries shared with the team
- Figma/design tool links in issue descriptions and PRs
- Collaboration during sprint planning to clarify UX acceptance criteria

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master facilitates agile ceremonies, helps remove blockers, and promotes agile best practices across the team. They act as a servant leader to keep delivery on track and foster continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and actively remove blockers that impede team progress
- Coach the team on agile principles and practices
- Protect the team from scope creep and unplanned interruptions
- Coordinate with Project Manager to track delivery cadence
- Track and report on team velocity and sprint health metrics

### Goals
- Enable a high-performing, self-organizing team
- Reduce cycle time and minimize delivery blockers
- Support a culture of continuous improvement

### Typical Communication
- Leads and facilitates all agile ceremonies
- Daily coordination with Project Manager and Developers on blockers
- Retrospective action items and follow-up tracking
- Team health check-ins and capacity planning discussions

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They analyze requirements, translate stakeholder needs into actionable user stories, and clarify acceptance criteria for the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and validate business and functional requirements
- Translate business needs into user stories with clear acceptance criteria
- Collaborate with Product Managers to refine and prioritize the backlog
- Facilitate requirement workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Identify process gaps and propose improvements
- Validate solutions against business requirements during QA

### Goals
- Ensure the team builds the right thing to meet business needs
- Reduce ambiguity and rework caused by unclear requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between technical and non-technical stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Requirement workshops and backlog refinement sessions with Product Managers
- User story reviews and acceptance criteria walkthroughs with Developers
- Regular check-ins with Stakeholders to validate requirements
- Documentation of business rules and process flows in the project wiki or docs

---

## Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Stakeholders represent business, operational, or client interests in the project. They provide requirements, feedback, and approvals throughout the project lifecycle, and are key participants at decision gates.

### Responsibilities
- Provide clear business requirements and desired outcomes at project initiation
- Participate in decision gates and milestone reviews
- Review and approve deliverables and release readiness
- Provide timely feedback on prototypes, demos, and release candidates
- Escalate business-critical risks or blockers to the Project Manager
- Champion the project within their area of the organization

### Goals
- Ensure the project delivers expected business value
- Maintain visibility into progress and risks without being in day-to-day delivery
- Support the team with timely decisions and approvals

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates from the Project Manager
- Milestone and demo reviews
- Decision gate participation (go/no-go approvals)
- Ad-hoc escalation as needed for business-impacting issues
